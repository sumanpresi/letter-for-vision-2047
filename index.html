1. Context: Why this letter exists

India at a turning point

India approaches 100 years of Independence in 2047, with rising demands on mineral security, climate resilience, clean energy, and strategic autonomy.

Role of GSI

As the nation’s premier geoscientific institution, GSI provides foundational inputs for resources, hazards, infrastructure, and long-term planning.

Global pressures

Critical minerals, Net Zero goals, data transparency, and geopolitical competition require a future-ready geoscience system.

The letter is not a directive. It is a consultative call inviting inputs, suggestions, and perspectives to strengthen the Vision 2047 roadmap.

3. What the Vision 2047 document contains

Vision & Mission (Why)

Positioning GSI as a world-class, innovative, agile, multidisciplinary geoscience institution by 2047.

Strategic Pillars (How)

Seven pillars defining transformation across minerals, technology, public good, data, people, partnerships, and policy.

Action Framework (When)

Clearly defined short, medium, long-term, and 2047 goals across all geoscience domains.

Global Alignment (With whom)

Adoption of global best practices, FAIR data principles, and international partnerships.

6. What is expected from stakeholders

Type of input

Feedback, suggestions, and perspectives based on domain expertise and operational experience.

Focus areas

Critical minerals, natural hazards, clean energy, Net Zero goals, data systems, and institutional capacity.

Why it matters

The final Vision must be realistic, inclusive, and institutionally owned, not top-down.

7. The deeper message

Vision 2047 is not owned by one division or office. The consultation signals maturity, openness, and a shift from execution-only thinking to long-term institutional leadership.

In essence:
Vision 2047 is a shared blueprint. This letter invites the institution to think together before it acts together.
